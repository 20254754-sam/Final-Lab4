Gyeongbokgung Palace, located in Seoul, is South Koreaâ€™s number one tourist attraction. It dates back to the 12th century and was the royal palace during the Joseon dynasty. Visitors can explore its grand architecture, royal halls, and beautiful gardens, offering a glimpse into Korea's royal history and culture.

Bulguksa Temple in Gyeongju is a UNESCO World Heritage Buddhist temple from the Silla Kingdom era. It is known for its historic architectural beauty and two bronze Buddha statues, making it a key site for those interested in Korean history and spirituality. Gyeongju, historically the ancient capital, also offers other cultural treasures like the Daereungwon Tomb Complex and the Gyeongju National Museum.

Busan, on the southern coast, is famed for its vibrant maritime culture. It is home to South Korea's largest and busiest port and features beautiful beaches like Haeundae Beach, cultural villages such as the Gamcheon Culture Village known for colorful street art, and significant temples like Haedong Yonggungsa by the ocean.
